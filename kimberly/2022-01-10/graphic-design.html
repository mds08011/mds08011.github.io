Graphic Design Software Recommendations

January 10, 2022

Introduction

Kimberly, I am initially recommending some Free and Open Source (FOSS) graphic design software to you. But hear me out! Unlike the weird-ass software you normally see me using, these are all very established software projects, with large communities, and ample free documentation and help available online.

Inkscape

Inkscape is a popular graphics design software: https://inkscape.org/

GIMP

GNU Image Manipulation Program (GIMP) - https://www.gimp.org/ - is another piece of free and open software popular in graphic design. This is typically more for editing photos (and other "raster" images). At a high level you can think of Inkscape as an alternative to Illistrator, and GIMP as an alternative to Photoshop.

It is more complicated than that, and I would like to vervisit this when I have time, but in the meantime I will punt explaining the differences to Nick Saporito: https://logosbynick.com/inkscape-vs-gimp-complete-comparison/

Krita

If you'd like to get into free-form digital painting, Krita (https://krita.org/en/) is more suited to that. I have never used this program myself but have heard good things.
